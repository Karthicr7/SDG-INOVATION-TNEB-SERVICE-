Government Solar Panel Schemes in India

India’s government is strongly promoting solar energy adoption through various schemes aimed at making solar power affordable and accessible for households, farmers, and businesses. These initiatives offer subsidies, incentives, and support to increase sustainability and reduce dependence on fossil fuels.

Key Schemes and Benefits

1. PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana

Objective: To provide free or subsidized rooftop solar panels to millions of residential households.

  • Subsidies of up to ₹78,000 for 3 kW rooftop solar systems.
  • Free electricity units (up to 300 units/month) generated from the solar system.
  • Target Group: Middle and lower-income groups.
  • Application: Eligible households can apply online through the official portal.

2. PM KUSUM (Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan)

Designed for farmers to adopt solar-powered pumps and decentralize power generation.

  • Provides financial assistance and feed-in tariffs to encourage farmers to generate and sell solar power.
  • Enables reduction of grid dependence and promotes agriculture sustainability.

3. National Rooftop Solar Programme

  • Offers subsidies ranging from 20-40% of the installation cost for rooftop solar systems.
  • Includes net metering facilities allowing consumers to feed excess power into the grid and earn credits.
  • Applicable for residential and commercial consumers, promoting grid-connected solar adoption.

4. Net Metering and Net Billing Policies

  • Enables consumers with rooftop solar panels to export excess energy to the state grid.
  • Ensures fair compensation via credits or payments, reducing overall electricity costs.
  • Widely adopted by states to encourage clean energy use.

5. Subsidies for MSMEs and Commercial Users

  • Special tax incentives and accelerated depreciation benefits for commercial installations.
  • Subsidies support small and medium businesses to upgrade to solar power, lowering operational costs and carbon footprint.

Additional Incentives

  • Waiver of electricity duty on solar power consumption.
  • Priority sector lending and low-interest loans for solar projects through various financial schemes.
  • Support for setting up solar parks and manufacturing units under national renewable energy goals.

How to Apply

Visit the official Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE) portal. Check eligibility criteria and subsidy norms based on your consumer category.

Register and submit online applications through state DISCOM or MNRE portals. Get approvals, instalment details, and technical support from authorized vendors.

Why Adopt Solar?

  • Significant reduction in electricity bills and long-term savings.
  • Contribution towards India’s clean energy and climate commitments.
  • Increased energy security and independence.
  • Enhanced property value with renewable technology upgrades.

For detailed application procedures, latest subsidy updates, and state-wise schemes, visit the official MNRE website or contact your local electricity board.
